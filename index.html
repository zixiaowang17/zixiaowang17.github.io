Welcome! My name is Zixiao [/tsʰɨ́.ɕjɑʊ̯/] Jolene Wang. I am a 2nd year Ph.D. student at Harvard University, co-advised by James Robins and Rajarshi Mukherjee.

Previously, I received my M.S. in Biostatistics from Johns Hopkins University advised by Ilya Shpitser and Hongkai Ji, and my B.S. from Shanghai Jiao Tong University advised by Lin Liu and Hongyu Zhao.

Research Topics

I am interested in high-dimensional statistics, causal inference, reinforcement learning, and theorem proving. My current focus is on improving the quality and structure of mathematical reasoning data, especially with Lean 4. I am also exploring how to collaborate efficiently through formal systems and how to provide reliable reasoning.
